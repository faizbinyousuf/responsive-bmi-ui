What your BMI result means

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 is considered a healthy weight for adults. BMI is not the sole determinant of a healthy weight range, but it provides a valuable starting point to evaluate your overall health and well-being. Aim for a nutritionally balanced diet and regular physical activity to maintain a healthy BMI. Additionally, strive for a healthy lifestyle, such as a balanced diet, regular exercise, and sufficient sleep, to support your overall health and well-being.

Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is a useful indicator of overall health, it has limitations when used to diagnose specific health conditions. Specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcome, and in certain cases, consider additional factors such as lifestyle choices..

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI.

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is advisable to minimise health risks for both mother and child.

Race

Certain health concerns may affect individuals of some Black and Asian origins at lower BMIs than others. To learn more, it is advised to discuss this with your GP or practice nurse.
